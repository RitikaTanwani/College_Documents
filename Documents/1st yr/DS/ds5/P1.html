H Index of Researcher

Question

One measure that is gaining popularity recently for researcher effectiveness is the h-index.
Read about it on wikepedia.
In this problem, we compute a slightly new mesaure as follows :
Conisder a graph with two kinds of nodes: author nodes corresponding to authors, and paper nodes that correspond to papers.
There are directed edges from an author node A to all the papers that are authored/co-authored by A.
There are also directed edges between papers such that an edge from P1 to P2 indicates that paper P2 cites paper P1.
Given this graph, for each author find his h-index defined as follows :
An author is said to have a h- index of k if exactly k of his papers have at least k citations and the remaining papers have at most k citations each.
Under the above definition, find the h-index of all authors in the input.

Input Specification

First Line : Number of Authors (A)
Second Line : Number of Papers (P)
Third Line : Number of Author-Paper Tuples (M)
Fourth Line : Number of Paper-Paper Tuples (N)
This is followed by M lines of Author-Paper tuples and then N lines of Paper-Paper tuples.
Author 1 will be denoted as a1, Author 2 as a2 and so on
Paper 1 will be denoted as p1, Paper 2 will be denoted as p2 and so on

Output Specification

Output the h-index of each author on separate line, starting from author 1, then author 2 and so on till the last author.

Sample Input

3
3
4
2
a1 p1
a2 p2
a3 p3
a2 p3
p2 p3
p3 p1

Sample Output

0
1
1
